# Bronze Criteria for Research Computing Infrastructure teams

## Green DiSC Representative

![Category general](https://img.shields.io/badge/category-general-blue)
![Criteria ID](https://img.shields.io/badge/Criteria%20ID-(CT)%20G1--B-white)

Nomination of a Green DiSC representative that is responsible for this Green DiSC submission and other sustainability efforts in the RCI team.

### Impact
Ensures there is a lead taking ownership of this Green DiSC submission and other sustainability efforts in the RCI team.

### Why?
Continuity is crucial to all sustainability initiatives, and is the sign of a truly successful effort. This ensures that someone is nominated (and a replacement is identified if they leave) to carry forward the targets laid out in this framework.

### How?
A member of staff takes on the responsibility, and makes sure to pass it on when they leave. When initiating a sustainability initiative, they make any relevant sustainability or facilities teams aware. They can sometimes provide support or guidance.

### Checklist
- Nominate a Green DiSC representative from your group and ensure they understand the responsibilities involved
- Document their nomination as the Green DiSC representative (e.g. include in job description, institutional profile page)
- Include their contact details in this documentation (i.e. institutional email)


## Computing sustainability in induction procedures for the RCI team

![Category general](https://img.shields.io/badge/category-general-blue)
![Criteria ID](https://img.shields.io/badge/Criteria%20ID-(CT)%20G2--B-white)

There is induction material about digital sustainability available to all new starters in the RCI team. This induction material should highlight how the team embeds environmental sustainability in their practice. 

### Impact
Emphasises the importance of adopting efficient and sustainable practices from day one.

### Why?
While there may be a substantial amount of information for new starters, it is crucial that environmental sustainability is emphasised on arrival. It is during this period of initiation that people are most impressionable, despite there being a lot of new information.

### How?
This refers to content specific about digital and computing sustainability, not generic sustainability efforts. Sustainability induction procedures can be developed by the RCI team or inspired from examples in other institutions (no need to reinvent the wheel!). For reference, some resources shared by other Green DiSC applicants can be found in the [ESCS Forum](https://forum.escs-community.org/c/green-disc/9). Sustainable practices should not be separate, but rather fully integrated into induction materials. 

### Checklist
- Include computing sustainability in induction material
- Attach the relevant induction material in your submission
- Detail how this material is disseminated and how it is made accessible to new starters


## Repository for green computing information

![Category general](https://img.shields.io/badge/category-general-blue)
![Criteria ID](https://img.shields.io/badge/Criteria%20ID-(CT)%20G3--B-white)

The RCI team maintains a repository (e.g. on the intranet) with useful green computing-related information for their users, and a list of the computing infrastructures they manage. In addition to RCI users, these are shared with the institution’s central sustainability and IT teams, and with other RCI teams within the institution (if any).

### Impact
Ensures relevant resources are centrally located and easily accessible for updating and dissemination.

### Why?
Sharing resources is a great way to avoid duplicating efforts and increase adoption.

### How?
A repository of digital sustainability-related information and a list of computing infrastructures is maintained on a central sustainability platform, and this is shared to your users.

### Checklist
- Document where the repository is located, what information it contains, and if possible how many have access
- Document how these resources are shared and made accessible to other groups


## Green DiSC dissemination

![Category general](https://img.shields.io/badge/category-general-blue)
![Criteria ID](https://img.shields.io/badge/Criteria%20ID-(CT)%20G4--B-white)

The Green DiSC scheme is not only implemented within the RCI team, but also disseminated across the user community. This includes sharing the Green DiSC criteria and resources, updating the community about the status of the application/certification, and making evidence accessible to encourage other applications from central teams and research groups within the user community.

### Impact
Increase adoption, strengthen sustainability initiatives, and avoid duplicating efforts.

### Why?
Aside from shared resources, sharing sustainability updates and best practices encourages engagement and helps grow and strengthen the user community within your institution.

### How?
Through engagement with users (e.g. meetings, mailing lists, department policies, etc.).

### Checklist
- Document how the RCI team engages with the user community to disseminate this program
- Provide evidence of these promotion activities undertaken


## Documented “Reuse, Repair, Recycle” process for computing hardware 

![Category general](https://img.shields.io/badge/category-general-blue)
![Criteria ID](https://img.shields.io/badge/Criteria%20ID-(CT)%20G5--B-white)

There is documentation of how broken, spare, and unusable equipment (i.e. electronic waste) from the RCI team is dealt with. As much as possible, broken equipment is repaired, spare equipment is reallocated, and any electronic waste is recycled. This covers office computers, laptops, monitors, and hard drives used for work, as well as servers equipment and other related equipment and parts.

### Impact
Understand what your team’s and institution's policies are for minimising and recycling e-waste from your team.

### Why?
Non-recycled e-waste is a rapidly growing issue across the world, and electronic devices piling up in dump sites – often in low income countries – are responsible for serious health consequences. 

### How?
List the disposing streams available from institution guidelines, as well as any more specific best-practices implemented by your team. These processes can include internal reuse, external reuse, recycling,  resale through external vendors, and processing any electronic waste through take-back schemes.

### Checklist
- List the different processes in place to Reuse, Repair, Recycle electronic hardware

## Inventory of office computing hardware

![Category general](https://img.shields.io/badge/category-offices-yellow)
![Criteria ID](https://img.shields.io/badge/Criteria%20ID-(CT)%20O1--B-white)

There is a list of the different computing hardware owned by the RCI team in the office, whether or not it is currently in use, as well as suppliers and warranty policies that apply. This list is updated yearly and includes laptops, desktop computers, monitors, tablets, external hard drives, and testing equipment (if any). This does not include server rooms hardware, which is a separate criteria.

### Impact
This helps monitor the equipment the team owns already, ensure that none gets forgotten about, and will later on enable assessment of the hardware's environmental impact (e.g. manufacturing and end-of-life treatment).

### Why?
Embodied carbon, or the emissions associated with the manufacturing, transportation, installation, maintenance, and end-of-life stages of a product, can take up a significant portion of emissions for equipment. For desktops and laptops, manufacturing takes up about 80% of the total life cycle emissions of the product – greatly outweighing the environmental impacts of the usage phase.

### How?
List the hardware owned by the RCI team by linking with colleagues. Where data is missing, this should be clearly highlighted so that it can be revisited in the future. In the case where a personal device is used significantly for work (e.g. using your own laptop), then this may be included for monitoring purposes. Unused equipment (e.g. spare or unusable equipment or parts) should also be listed and identified as such. Include information on actions to be taken for each, and information on how to interact with asset management systems, if available.

### Checklist
Inventory the relevant computing hardware equipment used by each member of your group
- Laptops (i.e. brand, model, general specs)
- External monitors (e.g. size)
- External hard drives (e.g. SSD, HDD)
Populate an inventory of these hardware in use
- Indicate the owner
- Indicate the quantity per item
- (Optional) Indicate the location, if office/storage is spread out
- Indicate if it’s currently in use

You may use [this template](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1GEUPeHO6CnJhCOhJHdSKYH6c0cKhPyXy3UUZzWpv7WI/edit?gid=0#gid=0) created by a previous awardee as reference, just download a copy if you would like to adopt the table(s).


## Inventory of data resources managed by the RCI team

![Category general](https://img.shields.io/badge/category-data%20storage-red)
![Criteria ID](https://img.shields.io/badge/Criteria%20ID-(CT)%20DS1--B-white)

There is an inventory of the main data resources directly managed by the RCI team, including key information about each dataset (storage location, number of users, size, whether the data is unique or duplicate of another repository, number of backups, etc.). This inventory is updated regularly (frequency depends on field, at least yearly).

### Impact
Taking stock of large data resources being used is needed to understand and reduce the associated environmental impacts.

### Why?
Identifying all large datasets is a crucial first step to finding ways to mitigate the associated impact on the environment.

### How?
List the main data resources managed by the RCI team. “main”refers to significance in terms of size or difficulty to replace. Include any available information: storage location, number of users, size, whether the data is unique or duplicate of another repository, number of backups, etc.

## Data cleaning process and user education

![Category general](https://img.shields.io/badge/category-data%20storage-red)
![Criteria ID](https://img.shields.io/badge/Criteria%20ID-(CT)%20DS3--B-white)

There is a clear and regularly occurring process (twice a year is ideal) for the cleaning of data directories owned by the RCI team. This process should include regular reminders for deletion of temporary/outdated files. Data cleaning best practices should also be made available to the user community.

### Impact
To avoid unnecessary accumulation of data that may no longer be needed or relevant and hoarding valuable space in storage.

### Why?
Data directories tend to accumulate temporary files that hoard space unnecessarily.

### How?
Enforce a regularly occurring data cleaning process for data owned by the team, in coordination with central IT teams and users. For a guide on organising data cleaning in your team, you can check out this [resource](https://journals.plos.org/ploscompbiol/article?id=10.1371/journal.pcbi.1013791). For best practice, you can use [this resource as a DMP template](https://dmponline.dcc.ac.uk/), but there are many other options available online.

### Checklist
- Recall and document when the last data cleaning occured
  - Description of the scope covered and storage freed
  - Include detail on the frequency and next scheduled cleaning, if available
- Provide evidence of the data cleaning process being disseminated (e.g. screenshots, emails)
- Provide evidence of the data cleaning best practices (e.g. screenshots, PDFs)

## Inventory of data centres managed by the RCI team

![Category general](https://img.shields.io/badge/category-compute-silver)
![Criteria ID](https://img.shields.io/badge/Criteria%20ID-(CT)%20RCI1--B-white)

There is a list of the computing infrastructures (i.e. buildings, data centres) managed by the RCI team, including both on-site (e.g. local data centres, servers in the office) and off-site infrastructures (e.g. remote institutional computing facilities). This inventory is updated regularly (frequency depends on field, at least yearly). It is recommended to coordinate with any central IT teams and the IT estate to ensure that this list is comprehensive.

### Impact
Understand the current services used by the institution as well the electricity sources of each service.

### Why?
The first step to addressing the impact of computing is to have an understanding of the usage, the infrastructures being managed by the institution, and the sources of electricity to power them. This can enable long-term improvements, carbon accounting, and support future sustainability policies.

### How?
This inventory should at least include, for each site: location, Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE), cooling technology used, and information on the electricity sources of each infrastructure (i.e. either main power grid, most common, or dedicated power source such as a solar farm). Power purchase agreements can be mentioned, but don’t replace listing the primary source . Noteworthy energy efficiency technology implemented can be included in this inventory. Cloud providers are not included in this scope, as they will not be managed by your team.

### Checklist
- List of the data infrastructures managed by the RCI team, with appropriate specs (e.g. on-site or off-site, electricity source)

## Inventory of server hardware

![Category general](https://img.shields.io/badge/category-compute-silver)
![Criteria ID](https://img.shields.io/badge/Criteria%20ID-(CT)%20RCI2--B-white)

There is an inventory of all computing hardware used in server rooms, including servers, computing cores, storage, etc. This only covers data centres (or parts of data centres in case of shared infrastructures) within the responsibility of the RCI team.

### Impact
Helps track and monitor all the relevant server hardware that is being used, and will later on enable assessment of the hardware's environmental impact (e.g. manufacturing and end-of-life treatment).

### Why?
Listing hardware is important to accounting for their impacts and calibrate future procurement. Embodied carbon, or the emissions associated with the manufacturing, transportation, installation, maintenance, and end-of-life stages of a product, can take up a significant portion of emissions for equipment. For servers, this takes up about 20% of the total life cycle emissions of the product. However, for desktops and laptops, manufacturing takes up about 80%, greatly outweighing the environmental impacts of the usage phase. 

### How?
List the computing hardware in server rooms falling in these categories (at least): servers, computing cores, storage units. This does not need to include things like switches and power distribution units (PDU). This inventory is updated regularly (frequency depends on field, at least yearly).

### Checklist
- List the computing hardware used in server rooms

## Inventory of third-party computing providers

![Category general](https://img.shields.io/badge/category-compute-silver)
![Criteria ID](https://img.shields.io/badge/Criteria%20ID-(CT)%20RCI3--B-white)

There is a list of the largest/most active computing infrastructures used by users in your institution but not managed by the RCI team that you are aware of and/or interact with. Data on these providers can of course be difficult to obtain (as not operated by the RCI team), so the focus here is on what information can feasibly be collected, and focusing on the major providers (e.g. largest financial contracts) can be a good way to go.

### Impact
Understand the current services used by users in the  institution and include them in sustainability strategies.

### Why?
The first step to addressing the impact of computing is to have an understanding of all usage, including the third-party services being used. This can enable long-term improvements, carbon accounting, and support future sustainability policies.

### How?
This includes infrastructures used by the user community but owned by third-party providers. Include both on-site (if any) and off-site (e.g. remote computing facilities and commercial cloud providers) options and information on the electricity sources of each infrastructure (either main power grid or dedicated facility such as a solar farm). This should also include computing infrastructure used for teaching, if feasible. This inventory is updated regularly (frequency depends on field, at least yearly).

### Checklist
- List the data infrastructures used by the departments that are *not* managed by the RCI team, with appropriate specs and details (e.g. on-site or off-site, electricity source).

## Inventory of centrally installed software

![Category general](https://img.shields.io/badge/category-compute-silver)
![Criteria ID](https://img.shields.io/badge/Criteria%20ID-(CT)%20RCI4--B-white)

There is an inventory of all software that is centrally installed on the RCI, and this inventory is made available to users (either directly on the RCI system or in the documentation).

### Impact
Understand the current software and hardware requirements of the institution to enable long-term improvements, carbon accounting, and support future sustainability policies.

### Why?
Software directly influences how efficiently hardware and digital infrastructure use energy and resources.

### How?
Include information on the software version, key dependencies, hardware requirements, and other required specs. This inventory is updated regularly (frequency depends on field, at least yearly). An example of centrally installed software is [here](https://bear-apps.bham.ac.uk/).

## Inventory of computing workflow hotspots

![Category general](https://img.shields.io/badge/category-compute-silver)
![Criteria ID](https://img.shields.io/badge/Criteria%20ID-(CT)%20RCI5--B-white)

There is an inventory of the biggest resource users (e.g. based on number of jobs, longest jobs, highest total CPU time, most memory used) on the RCI. It can be individual users with particularly high usage, or the most popular widely used pipelines.

### Impact
Understanding the current computing pipelines run by the user community can enable long-term improvements, carbon accounting, and support future sustainability policies.

### Why?
The first step to addressing the impact of computing is to have an understanding of the usage. This can allow long-term comparisons and sustainability strategies to be addressed, and alerts facilities that sustainability is a priority.

### How?
Identify the computing workflow hotspots in your user community, and link with them to understand these workflows more and get additional information for the inventory. This inventory is updated regularly (frequency depends on field, at least yearly).

### Checklist
- List the most demanding computing workflows ran on the nodes and its biggest users
- Include detail on metric used for demand (e.g. runtime, number of resources, number times run)

## Overall energy monitoring of data centres

![Category general](https://img.shields.io/badge/category-compute-silver)
![Criteria ID](https://img.shields.io/badge/Criteria%20ID-(CT)%20RCI6--B-white)

Overall energy monitoring of the on-site server rooms (i.e. aggregate consumption for the entire room/building that the RCI team controls) and colocation data centre environments are monitored on a regular basis. This does not include remote cloud resources.

### Impact
To understand and monitor the overall energy usage of the data centres.

### Why?
To enable better management of resources and provide a reference for reducing carbon emissions, improving energy efficiency, and planning for more sustainability infrastructure.

### How?
This can be done through a number of ways, e.g. through existing  electricity meters for the buildings, or if not available, by aggregating the measurements of the servers PDUs.  

### Checklist
- Provide proof that overall energy monitoring is being conducted
- If available, include how this information is being reported, how this data is being used, and if there are targets to reduce or optimise this in the future
- (Optional) Include granular data of usage across time, if available
